# Tax Research Skill

When researching tax rules or answering tax questions, follow these guidelines.

## Research Approach

1. **Start with fundamentals**: Apply your knowledge of core tax concepts to frame the question.
2. **Search for current rules**: Tax law changes frequently. Always use web search to verify current rates, thresholds, phase-outs, and deadlines for the relevant tax year.
3. **Cite sources**: When referencing specific rules, note the source (IRS publication, tax code section, government website, etc.).
4. **Jurisdiction matters**: Always confirm which jurisdiction applies. Rules differ dramatically between countries, and between states/provinces within a country.

## Core Tax Concepts

### Income Classification
- **Ordinary income**: Wages, salaries, tips, interest, short-term capital gains, business income — taxed at regular rates
- **Qualified dividends and long-term capital gains**: Preferential lower rates in many jurisdictions
- **Tax-exempt income**: Municipal bond interest (US), certain foreign income under treaty, Roth distributions
- **Passive income vs. active income**: Different loss limitation rules apply

### Deductions vs. Credits
- **Deductions** reduce taxable income (value depends on marginal rate)
  - Above-the-line (available regardless of itemizing): IRA contributions, student loan interest, HSA contributions
  - Below-the-line (itemized): mortgage interest, state/local taxes (SALT), charitable contributions, medical expenses
- **Credits** reduce tax dollar-for-dollar (more valuable than deductions)
  - Nonrefundable: reduce tax to zero but no refund of excess
  - Refundable: can produce a refund beyond tax owed (e.g., Earned Income Credit, certain child tax credit portions)

### Marginal vs. Effective Tax Rates
- **Marginal rate**: The rate applied to the next dollar of income (the bracket you're "in")
- **Effective rate**: Total tax ÷ total income — the actual overall percentage paid
- Tax planning decisions should consider the **marginal rate** for timing and optimization

### Alternative Minimum Tax (AMT)
- A parallel tax calculation that disallows certain deductions and preferences
- Taxpayer pays the higher of regular tax or AMT
- Key triggers: large state/local tax deductions, incentive stock option exercises, tax-exempt private activity bond interest

### Estimated Tax Payments
- Required when withholding doesn't cover the tax liability
- Generally due quarterly (US: Apr 15, Jun 15, Sep 15, Jan 15)
- Safe harbor: pay 100% of prior year tax (110% for high-income taxpayers in the US) or 90% of current year tax
- Penalties for underpayment are calculated per quarter

## Key Research Areas

### Retirement Accounts
- **Contribution limits**: Change annually — always search for the current year's limits
- **Traditional vs. Roth**: Pre-tax vs. after-tax contributions; income limits for deductibility/eligibility
- **Required Minimum Distributions (RMDs)**: Age thresholds and calculation rules change with legislation
- **Employer plans vs. individual**: 401(k)/403(b) vs. Traditional/Roth IRA — different limits and rules
- **Catch-up contributions**: Additional amounts for those over 50 (and sometimes 60+)

### Capital Gains
- **Holding periods**: Short-term (≤ 1 year) vs. long-term (> 1 year)
- **Rate brackets**: Long-term capital gains have their own rate structure
- **Net Investment Income Tax (NIIT)**: Additional 3.8% on investment income above thresholds (US)
- **Capital loss limitations**: Usually limited to offsetting gains + a fixed amount per year (e.g., $3,000 in the US)
- **Carryforward rules**: Unused losses carry forward indefinitely in most jurisdictions

### Business and Self-Employment
- **Self-employment tax**: Social Security + Medicare on net self-employment income
- **Qualified Business Income (QBI) deduction**: Deduction for pass-through income (check current rules and limitations)
- **Home office deduction**: Simplified vs. regular method
- **Business expenses**: Ordinary and necessary; documentation requirements
- **Estimated taxes**: Self-employed individuals must make quarterly payments

### International
- **Foreign earned income exclusion**: For qualifying US citizens/residents abroad
- **Foreign tax credit**: Avoid double taxation on foreign-source income
- **FBAR / FATCA**: Reporting requirements for foreign financial accounts
- **Tax treaties**: May reduce withholding rates or exempt certain income types
- **Transfer pricing**: Rules for transactions between related entities across borders

### Real Estate
- **Depreciation**: Residential (27.5 years) vs. commercial (39 years) in the US
- **1031 like-kind exchanges**: Deferral of gain on investment property swaps
- **Primary residence exclusion**: Gain exclusion on sale of primary home (check current limits and requirements)
- **Passive activity rules**: Rental income is generally passive; real estate professional exception
- **Cost segregation**: Accelerating depreciation on components of a building

## Disclaimer Guidelines

Always include appropriate disclaimers:

- **Before detailed advice**: "This analysis is for informational purposes. Consult a qualified tax professional for advice specific to your situation."
- **When rules are ambiguous or changing**: "This area of tax law is subject to interpretation / pending legislation. Verify current rules with a tax professional."
- **When amounts are large or decisions are significant**: "Given the amounts involved, I strongly recommend consulting a CPA or tax attorney before proceeding."
- **When you're uncertain**: Clearly state what you know, what you're unsure about, and what the user should verify.
- **Never**: Claim to be providing legal or professional tax advice. Never guarantee a specific tax outcome.

## Search Tips

When searching for tax information:
- Include the **tax year** in your search (rules change annually)
- Use official source domains when possible (irs.gov, revenue.ie, gov.uk/hmrc, canada.ca/revenue-agency)
- Search for the specific **form number** or **code section** when applicable
- Look for "2025 tax brackets" rather than just "tax brackets" to get current data
- Check for recent legislation that may have changed long-standing rules
